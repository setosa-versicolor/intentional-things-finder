# Breweries & Beer Bars - Complete Content

## 1. Karben4 Brewing

**Story**: Known for malt bombs and hop grenades. The Fantasy Factory IPA is their most famous, but they also brew Ale Asylum's iconic beers like Hopalicious and Ambergeddon after acquiring them in 2023. East side institution since 2012.

**Nudge**: Taproom visit on a Saturday afternoon. Try a flight to figure out what you like. The outdoor space in summer is worth the trip alone.

**Tags**: friend-hangout, cheap-eats
**Vibe**: 0.4, 0.7, 0.5
**Location**: 3698 Kinsman Blvd
**Price**: 1

## 2. One Barrel Brewing

**Story**: Sticks to their mantra of small handmade batches despite exponential growth since 2012. Atwood Avenue neighborhood brewery that still feels like a neighborhood brewery.

**Nudge**: Weekend afternoon for the neighborhood vibe. Rotating taps mean you'll find something new each visit. Bring your own food or order delivery.

**Tags**: friend-hangout, cheap-eats
**Vibe**: 0.4, 0.7, 0.5
**Location**: 2001 Atwood Ave
**Price**: 1

## 3. Working Draft Beer Co.

**Story**: East side taproom with tasty drafts like Pulp Culture hazy IPA and Dad Bod oatmeal stout. The names tell you they don't take themselves too seriously, which makes the beer easier to enjoy.

**Nudge**: Thursday evening. Try the seasonal rotation. The space is small—get there early if you want a table. Food trucks rotate through.

**Tags**: friend-hangout, cheap-eats
**Vibe**: 0.4, 0.7, 0.5
**Location**: 1129 E Wilson St
**Price**: 1

## 4. Giant Jones Brewing

**Story**: Only certified organic brewery in Wisconsin. Woman-owned by Jessica and Erika Jones, started 2018 producing organic "big beers." Taproom hours are limited (Wed-Thu 4-8pm, Fri-Sat 4-9pm) which means they're brewing when they're not pouring.

**Nudge**: Friday evening. The organic commitment is real and tastes like it. Limited hours mean planning ahead, but worth it.

**Tags**: friend-hangout, cheap-eats, outside-my-norm
**Vibe**: 0.5, 0.7, 0.4
**Location**: 931 E. Main St, Suite 9
**Price**: 1

## 5. Delta Beer Lab

**Story**: Community destination brewery opened February 2019. Large taproom with steel-tip darts, shuffleboard, board games. Committed to social change, queer-owned. The kind of place that believes beer spaces should be inclusive, then builds the space to prove it.

**Nudge**: Weekend afternoon for the games and crowd. Bring friends. The beer is good but the atmosphere is why you're here.

## 7. Vintage Brewing Company

## 8. New Glarus Brewing Company

**Story**: Independently owned craft brewery in New Glarus (south of Madison) whose products are only found in Wisconsin. Founded 1993. Spotted Cow farmhouse ale is the best-selling, $21M Bavarian-village-style facility offers free self-guided tours.

**Nudge**: Worth the 30-minute drive. Do the self-guided tour first, then the tasting room. Buy a case to bring home—you can't get it anywhere else.

**Tags**: friend-hangout, educational, outside-my-norm
**Vibe**: 0.5, 0.6, 0.4
**Location**: New Glarus
**Price**: 1
