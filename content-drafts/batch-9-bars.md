# Neighborhood Bars & Dive Bars - Complete Content

## 1. Le Tigre Lounge

**Story**: Tucked beneath the Highway 14 overpass in a strip mall, this 60-year-old family-run lounge hasn't changed since Mad Men days and doesn't want to. Every surface covered in tiger decor, no TVs, strict no-swearing policy enforced by owners who still tend bar.

**Nudge**: Only open Thursday through Saturday. Order the ice-cold martini—they're experts. The jukebox is vinyl records. Don't swear, seriously.

**Tags**: friend-hangout, date-night, outside-my-norm
**Vibe**: 0.7, 0.9, 0.3
**Location**: West side strip mall
**Price**: 2

## 2. Mickey's Tavern

**Story**: Willy Street institution since 1902, formerly the Schubert Hotel. Kitschy and eclectic LGBTQA-friendly dive that does brunch the morning after live music nights. One of the most memorable bars on the east side for good reason.

**Nudge**: Weekend nights for live music. Sunday brunch to recover. The eclectic decor is part of the experience—embrace it.

**Tags**: friend-hangout, outside-my-norm, food-focused
**Vibe**: 0.3, 0.9, 0.6
**Location**: Willy Street
**Price**: 1

## 3. Genna's Lounge

**Story**: Beloved downtown institution that's divey without being dirty. Stays open until 2am, serves the cheapest happy hour in town with free chips, salsa, and meatballs. Nightly events including trivia and karaoke keep it packed.

**Nudge**: Happy hour for the free food and cheap drinks. Late night when everything else closes. Karaoke if you're brave or drunk enough.

## 4. Caribou Tavern

**Story**: Classic neighborhood bar in the same family's hands since the '70s. Famous for the 'Bou Burger—extra-large beef patty perfectly cooked with cheese and all the extras. The kind of place where regulars have their own spots.

**Nudge**: Go for the burger, stay for a second drink. Weeknight dinner avoids the rush. Sit at the bar if you're solo.

## 5. Laurel Tavern

**Story**: Authentic Wisconsin neighborhood tavern on Monroe Street since 1969. Famous Friday fish fry with walleye that's worth the wait, Saturday prime rib. Popular with Edgewood and West High alums who never really left the neighborhood.

**Nudge**: Friday fish fry—get there early or make a reservation. The Laurel burger any other night. Sit in the dining room, not the bar, if you want conversation.

**Tags**: friend-hangout, food-focused
**Vibe**: 0.4, 0.9, 0.5

## 6. Crystal Corner Bar
