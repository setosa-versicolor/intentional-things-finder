# Parks - Complete Content

## 1. James Madison Park

**Story**: Prime lakefront location featuring the Gates of Heaven synagogue building, moved here in 1971 and now a designated City landmark on the National Register of Historic Places. Best downtown lake access.

**Nudge**: Sunset on the pier. Bring nothing, leave your plans at home. The synagogue building is worth looking at, not just looking past.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.7, 0.2, 0.3
**Location**: 614 E. Gorham St
**Price**: 0

---

## 2. Tenney Park

**Story**: Multiple playgrounds, bridges over lagoon, beach, pier into Lake Mendota with great views. Watch boats come through the locks—oddly meditative. Friendly atmosphere, storybook scenery. Ice skate rentals and concessions when staffed.

**Nudge**: Walk the full loop around the park first (15 min), then end at the lock. Bring nothing. Watch the water. Summer evenings for kayakers and herons.

**Tags**: nature, free, kid-friendly, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.6, 0.3, 0.4
**Location**: Lake Mendota shore
**Price**: 0

---

## 3. Olbrich Botanical Gardens

**Story**: The Thai Pavilion catches most visitors, but the real magic is the rock garden path in late afternoon light. 16-acre outdoor garden plus conservatory. Voted one of top 10 most inspiring gardens in North America. Free outdoor gardens.

**Nudge**: Enter through the back parking lot. Turn left immediately. Walk slowly. The bench by the pond is worth sitting on for ten minutes. Adjacent Biergarten in summer.

**Tags**: nature, free, kid-friendly, outside-my-norm, educational
**Vibe**: 0.8, 0.4, 0.2
**Location**: 3330 Atwood Ave
**Price**: 0

---

## 4. Henry Vilas Park

**Story**: One of Madison's oldest parks on northern shore of Lake Wingra. Sandy beach, accessible fishing piers, two playgrounds including famous Old Woman in a Shoe slide. Third playground with dinosaur equipment near large Indian mound. Home to free Henry Vilas Zoo.

**Nudge**: Weekday afternoon avoids weekend crowds. Start at the zoo (free), walk to the beach, end at the dinosaur playground. The Indian mound on the hill is worth the climb.

**Tags**: nature, free, kid-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.4, 0.3, 0.6
**Location**: Northern shore of Lake Wingra
**Price**: 0

---

## 5. Warner Park

**Story**: Largest park in Madison with 250+ acres. Best sledding hill in Madison. Summer splash park. The scale gives you room to actually get lost, which is rare in city parks.

**Nudge**: Winter for sledding—the hill earns its reputation. Summer for splash park with kids. Spring/fall for the trails when it's empty.

**Tags**: nature, free, kid-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.5, 0.2, 0.7
**Location**: North side Madison
**Price**: 0

---

## 6. Elver Park

**Story**: Largest community park in Madison with 200+ acres. Big playground, hiking trails, winter ice skating and cross-country skiing (rentals available), huge sledding hill, summer splash pad. Disc golf with holes 2-4 redesigned and opened 2025.

**Nudge**: Winter sledding hill rivals Warner's. Summer splash pad for kids. Fall hiking when leaves are turning. Disc golf if you throw.

**Tags**: nature, free, kid-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.4, 0.1, 0.7
**Location**: West Madison
**Price**: 0

---

## 7. Hoyt Park

**Story**: Former quarry with unique character. Picnic shelters with stone fireplaces, winding wooded trails with stunning sunset views. One of few places downtown for campfires.

**Nudge**: Evening for the sunset. The quarry walls make it distinct. Bring firewood if you want a campfire. The trails loop but let you actually wander.

**Tags**: nature, free, friend-hangout, outside-my-norm
**Vibe**: 0.7, 0.2, 0.4
**Location**: West side Madison
**Price**: 0

---

## 8. Olin Park

**Story**: Some of the best views of downtown Madison from Lake Monona shore. Convenient, accessible, affordable. Pavilion closed 2026 for essential repairs, but the shoreline is the real draw anyway.

**Nudge**: Sunset facing downtown. Bring a blanket, not a plan. The skyline view is Madison's postcard shot. Summer for Concerts on the Square overflow crowd.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.7, 0.2, 0.3
**Location**: Lake Monona shore
**Price**: 0

## 9. Brittingham Park

**Story**: Beach access, canoe/kayak rental, concessions, picnic tables, playground. Houses Camp Randall Rowing Club at historic Brittingham Boat House—oldest surviving park building in Madison. Lake Monona access with actual amenities.

**Nudge**: Kayak rental in summer—the lake is calmer than it looks. Playground for kids. The boat house is worth looking at. Beach swimming when it's hot.

**Tags**: nature, free, kid-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.5, 0.2, 0.6
**Location**: 701 W. Brittingham Pl
**Price**: 0

---

## 10. Owen Conservation Park

**Story**: 92-acre conservation park, former farm and retreat of UW Professor Edward T. Owen. Prairies and oak savannas reclaiming the landscape. 3.4 miles of trails featuring diverse habitats. West side hilltop location.

**Nudge**: Fall for the prairie colors. Spring for wildflowers. Go alone and walk the full 3.4 miles. The habitat transitions are the point.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly, educational
**Vibe**: 0.8, 0.1, 0.3
**Location**: Old Sauk Road
**Price**: 0

---

## 11. Pheasant Branch Conservancy

**Story**: Described as "best urban park and green space" in Wisconsin. Eagle nest viewable in spring. Diverse habitats for birdwatching, trails with changing scenery through prairies and woodlands.

**Nudge**: Spring morning for birds. Bring binoculars if you have them. The eagle nest is real and active. Early morning before dog walkers.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly, educational
**Vibe**: 0.8, 0.1, 0.3
**Location**: West Madison
**Price**: 0

## 12. Marshall Park

**Story**: 27 acres on west side of Lake Mendota, purchased 1956. Named for park system developer instrumental in adding Cherokee Marsh and developing Olbrich Botanical Gardens. Lake access without the crowds.

**Nudge**: Weekday afternoon. Lake Mendota views without the Picnic Point crowds. Quiet neighborhood feel. Bring a book or don't.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.7, 0.2, 0.3
**Location**: West side Lake Mendota
**Price**: 0
