# Nature & Trails - Complete Content

## 1. UW Arboretum

**Story**: 17+ miles of trails through restored prairies, savannas, woodlands, wetlands. Curtis Prairie Trail runs through one of the oldest restored prairies in the US. No bikes, no pets, no picnics, no drones—just trails and ecosystems.

**Nudge**: Go alone. Lakeshore Path entrance for easy access. Curtis Prairie in late summer for peak wildflowers. Leave your phone in your pocket until you actually need it.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly, educational
**Vibe**: 0.8, 0.1, 0.3
**Location**: Southwest Madison
**Price**: 0

## 2. Picnic Point

**Story**: A narrow peninsula stretching into Lake Mendota. The walk out and back is exactly long enough to process something you've been avoiding thinking about. Sunset from the point is Madison's best free show. Open sunrise to sunset.

**Nudge**: Go alone. Leave your phone in your pocket until you reach the tip. On the way back, you'll know what you needed to figure out.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.8, 0.1, 0.2
**Location**: UW-Madison Lakeshore Nature Preserve
**Price**: 0

## 3. Capital City State Trail

**Story**: 20+ miles of paved shared-use trail connecting Military Ridge Trail to E. Buckeye Road, passing through Fitchburg and downtown Madison. Passes Monona Terrace and Olbrich Gardens. State Trail Pass required ($25 annual/$5 daily) for ages 16+ on 9-mile section.

**Nudge**: Start at Monona Terrace, ride to Olbrich Gardens, grab coffee, ride back. The Frank Lloyd Wright architecture and botanical gardens in one ride. Fall colors are worth planning for.

**Tags**: nature, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.6, 0.1, 0.6
**Location**: 17+ miles through Madison
**Price**: 0

## 4. Cherokee Marsh - North Unit

**Story**: Trails explore restored prairie, oak savanna, woodland. Boardwalks with observation decks offering views of upper Yahara River, deep marsh, sedge meadow. The wetland vistas are what you came for.

**Nudge**: Early morning for birds. Spring and fall migrations bring variety. The boardwalk sections keep your feet dry and put you in the middle of the marsh.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly, educational
**Vibe**: 0.8, 0.1, 0.3
**Location**: North Madison
**Price**: 0

## 5. Lower Yahara River Trail

**Story**: 5.1 miles from Madison to McFarland, opened 2017. One of the longest non-motorized boardwalk bridges in North America with stunning Lake Waubesa views. Mile-long continuous bridge paralleling railroad.

**Nudge**: Bike it, don't walk it—5 miles is long on foot. The boardwalk bridge section is the highlight. Summer evening for lake views.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.7, 0.1, 0.5
**Location**: Madison to McFarland
**Price**: 0

## 6. Lakeshore Path

**Story**: Gravel and paved path alongside Lake Mendota connecting UW-Madison campus to Lakeshore Nature Preserve. Well-maintained, little elevation, beautiful lake views. The university walk that doesn't feel like a university walk.

**Nudge**: Morning or evening. Campus crowds during the day. The path transitions from campus to nature gradually. Keep walking past where most people turn around.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.6, 0.1, 0.4
**Location**: UW-Madison campus
**Price**: 0

## 7. Yahara River Bike Path

**Story**: Anchored by Tenney Park, connects to the 17-mile Capital City Trail. Views of the river along Yahara River Parkway. Northeast Madison connector route that's better than you'd expect.

**Nudge**: Bike path, not walking path. Connect from Tenney Park to Capital City Trail for a longer route. Summer for the full tree canopy.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.6, 0.1, 0.5
**Location**: Northeast Madison

## 8. Ice Age Trail Segments

**Story**: National Scenic Trail with segments near Madison. Ice Age Junction Trail passes through Ice Age Trail Junction Natural Resource Area and Badger Prairie County Park, connects to Military Ridge Trail. Glacial landscape still visible.

**Nudge**: Pick a segment, not the whole trail—it's 1,200 miles total. Cross Plains segment is closest. Fall colors. Bring proper hiking boots.

**Tags**: nature, free, solo-friendly, educational, outside-my-norm
**Vibe**: 0.7, 0.1, 0.5
**Location**: Various Madison area
**Price**: 0

## 9. Devil's Lake State Park

**Story**: About 1 hour north of Madison. Magnificent 500-foot quartzite bluffs overlooking 360-acre lake. Nearly 30 miles of hiking trails, beaches, paddling. Devil's Doorway formation hike is Wisconsin bucket list item. Bluffs carved by glaciers.

**Nudge**: East Bluff Trail to Devil's Doorway. Morning start to beat crowds. Bring water and real hiking shoes. The views earn the climb. State park pass required.

**Tags**: nature, educational, outside-my-norm
**Vibe**: 0.6, 0.1, 0.8
**Location**: 1 hour north of Madison
**Price**: 0

## 10. Governor Dodge State Park

**Story**: One of Wisconsin's largest parks, 5,000+ acres of steep hills, bluffs, deep valleys in the Driftless area. Two lakes and a waterfall. Military Ridge rail trail hugs southern border. Southwest Wisconsin's dramatic landscape.

**Nudge**: Twin Valley Lake for swimming. Mill Creek trail for the waterfall. Camping if you want to stay. Fall colors rival anywhere in Wisconsin. State park pass required.

**Tags**: nature, educational, outside-my-norm, kid-friendly
**Vibe**: 0.7, 0.1, 0.7
**Location**: Southwest of Madison
**Price**: 0
